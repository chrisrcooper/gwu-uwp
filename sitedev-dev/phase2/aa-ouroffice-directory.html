Featured Faculty

Emmanuel Teitelbaum

Does a Developing Democracy Benefit from Labor Repression?

Emmanuel Teitelbaum (Ph.D., Cornell University) is an Assistant Professor of Political Science and International Affairs at George Washington University. His expertise lies in comparative politics, South Asian politics, the political economy of development, and the political economy of labor. Professor Teitelbaum received his Ph.D. from Cornell University and his B.A. from John Carroll University in Cleveland, Ohio.

» Read more

Marie Price

Migrants to the Metropolis: The Rise of Immigrant Gateway Cities

Dr. Marie Price is an Associate Professor of Geography and International Affairs at the George Washington University, where she has taught since 1990. She is currently chair of the Department of Geography and directed the Latin American Studies Program from 1999-2001. A native of California, she earned her BA from the University of California at Berkeley (1984) and a Ph.D. from Syracuse University (1991).
