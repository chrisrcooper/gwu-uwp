About the Elliott School

Quick Links

The George Washington University has educated generations of international leaders and advanced the understanding of important global issues since 1821. The Elliott School of International Affairs, named in honor of former GW President Lloyd H. Elliott and his wife Evelyn, is dedicated to this mission.

Our cross-disciplinary faculty prepares Elliott School students to make a difference in the world by combining in-depth analysis with practical applications of global issues.

Our groundbreaking research institutes bring together renowned scholars to provide expert analyses and innovative solutions to complex debates.

Employers consistently choose Elliott School graduates for challenging global careers. Our Washington, DC location allows us to form strong relationships with public, private, and non-profit organizations that regularly employ Elliott School students and alumni.
