Color Palette

Print Resources

The core colors of GW's Graphic Identity System to be used on printed and electronic visual communications are Pantone® 302 blue, 1215 buff, 873 metallic gold, and 110 bright gold.

The GW Color Palette Guidelines

Web Color Palette

GW Blue

Pantone® 302 spot color

4-color process C100 M30 YO K50

Foundation of GW's graphic identity color palette. Recommended as a predominant color, and suitable for all uses, including 100% solid and full range of tints

GW Buff

Pantone® 1215 spot color

4-color process CO M6 Y43 KO

Recommended as a predominant color for backgrounds and accent color for graphic elements on light backgrounds. Not suitable for small type on light backgrounds.

GW Metallic Gold

Pantone® 873 metallic spot color

4-color process CO M20 Y85 K35

Metallic version is suitable for most uses. 4-color process build should only be used for very small accents. Use GW Bright Gold as indicated below for larger areas when metallic ink is not available or feasible.

GW Bright Gold

Pantone® 110 spot color

4-color process CO M20 Y100 K10

Use when metallic gold is not available or feasible--such as 4-color process printing, digital printing, or electronic media. Suitable for all uses, but avoid very small type on light backgrounds.

Pantone® 302 represents “GW Blue” as a fresh interpretation with historic precedent, distinguishing GW from other area universities. It provides outstanding performance whether rendered as solid or tints, across a full range of applications in both printed and electronic media. Varnishing is strongly recommended when printing large solids or darker tints in offset lithography.

“GW Buff” is represented by Pantone® 1215. This color is particularly effective when used as a background color or to accent large graphic elements. It also works well for type on dark backgrounds, but should not be used for small type on light backgrounds.

Pantone® 873 metallic gold is suitable for backgrounds, type on both light and dark backgrounds, and as an accent color. Interesting effects can be obtained by tinting, but tints above 60% should be avoided. Varnishing is strongly recommended when printing large solids or darker tints in offset lithography.

The 4-color process build is only to be used in situations where the spot metallic ink is not available, such as digital and electronic media, and should be restricted to very small accents (such as rules or frames) to mimic the metallic effect. The 4-color build is not suitable for large areas. Pantone® 873 metallic gold is not to be converted to a Pantone® non-metallic spot color.

Pantone® 110,“GW Bright Gold” is provided for all uses when a metallic effect cannot be achieved, such as 4-color process printing, digital printing, or electronic media. Avoid using for very small type on light backgrounds.

Black is considered neutral for the purposes of this system, but the use of black as a predominant background color should be avoided.

White paper is considered a neutral background. “GW Buff” can be effectively represented by papers in the off-white spectrum, with lighter shades recommended to minimize color shifting of inks.

For more information and guidance on appropriate color selection for various applications, contact GW Graphic Design at 202-994-0500 or graphics@gwu.edu.
