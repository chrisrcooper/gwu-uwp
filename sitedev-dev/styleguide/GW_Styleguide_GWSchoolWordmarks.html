GW School Wordmarks

Print Resources

School wordmarks are furnished to each school or college to use on its printed and electronic communications. All school wordmarks configure the name of the University and school or college consistently, reinforcing GW’s graphic identity.

The fundamental rules and regulations governing proper use of the GW wordmarks listed under “GW Wordmark System” and “Improper Uses of GW Wordmarks” apply to the school wordmarks as well.

GW School Wordmark Examples

The following rules are unique to the school wordmarks:

  • School wordmarks may not be used alone. Exceptions may be granted only for a strictly internal communication, such as a flyer posted on campus.
  • School wordmarks may only be used in advertisements when accompanied by an appropriate GW wordmark.
  • The engraved portrait version of the school wordmark is suitable only for print applications using high-quality methods and materials. It is not to be used for electronic communications, including websites.
  • A school may typeset its name on printed and electronic communications in lieu of using its school word-mark only when a GW wordmark appears prominently elsewhere, and the guidelines under “Use of School Wordmarks in Layouts” are followed.

When typesetting a school name, typographic techniques and devices that could serve to create the appearance of wordmarks are to be avoided. Examples include, but are not limited to:

  • Special characters or icons;
  • Stylized display fonts, forced alignments or baseline shifts
  • Very tight or loose letter spacing
  • Boxes, frames, or rules
  • Mixed sizes, weights, or fonts

Minion or Myriad, GW’s recommended serif and sans serif fonts, are the preferred fonts for typesetting school names. Requiem, the typeface upon which GW’s wordmarks are based, should not be used for this purpose in order to avoid any confusion with the official wordmarks.
